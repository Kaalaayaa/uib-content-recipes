Mamaliga

Ingredients

  • Cornmeal - 400g
  • Water - 1.5L
  • Salt - 1tsp
  • Butter or olive oil - 1tbsp

Preparation

  1. Traditionally, mămăligă is cooked by boiling water, salt and cornmeal in a special-shaped cast iron pot called ceaun or tuci
  2. When cooked peasant-style and used as a bread substitute, mămăligă is supposed to be much thicker than the regular Italian polenta to the point that it can be cut in slices, like bread.
  3. When cooked for other purposes, mămăligă can be much softer, sometimes almost to the consistency of porridge.
  4. Because mămăligă sticks to metal surfaces, a piece of sewing thread is used to cut it into slices instead of a knife; it can then be eaten by holding it with the hand, just like bread.
